## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience — engineering managers (weekly team health reviews), tech leads (daily code quality checks), and developers (self-monitoring). Context: professional, analytical, high-stakes decision-making. They need to trust the data they see.

### Brand Personality
Intelligent, transparent, trustworthy. Measured confidence — not flashy. The interface should feel like a colleague who always has the right answer, not a dashboard trying to impress.

### Aesthetic Direction
Move away from the current "AI slop" aesthetic (cyan/purple on black, glassmorphism, glow effects, Inter). Push toward a more distinctive, editorial data-dashboard feel inspired by Vercel and Datadog — precise typography, intentional whitespace, clear hierarchy. Possibly introduce a warmer neutral palette or a distinctive typographic personality. Light mode option should be considered.

Anti-references: pure "hacker dashboard" aesthetic, neon glow effects, glass cards, gradient text on metrics.

### Design Principles
1. **Data clarity over decoration** — every visual element should help users understand their codebase, not impress them
2. **Trustworthy precision** — sharp edges, confident typography, minimal noise; users are making real decisions
3. **Distinctive without being loud** — break from AI defaults through typographic and spatial choices, not color drama
4. **Progressive disclosure** — show key health signals first, reveal detail through interaction
5. **Consistent, predictable patterns** — engineers appreciate systems that behave as expected
